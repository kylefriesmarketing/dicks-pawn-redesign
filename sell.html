Good to Know

Pawn questions, answered

What's the difference between selling and pawning?

Selling means we buy your item outright — you get cash and we keep the item. Pawning is a collateral loan: you get cash now, we hold your item safe and insured, and you get it back when you repay the loan. Your choice, no pressure either way.

Do I need good credit?

No. Pawn loans are based on your item, not your credit. There's no credit check, and not repaying a pawn loan never affects your credit score — you'd simply forfeit the item.

What do I need to bring?

Just your item and a valid government-issued photo ID (required by South Carolina law for all pawn and buy transactions).

Is my item safe while it's pawned?

Yes — every pawned item is stored securely and fully insured until you pick it up. It's your property, and we treat it that way.

How do you decide what my item is worth?

Our staff appraise thousands of items a month. We look at brand, model, condition, completeness, and what the item genuinely resells for — then make you a fair, no-obligation offer on the spot.
